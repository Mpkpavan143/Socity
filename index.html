Screen Direction :

Screen direction, also known as camera direction, is the direction that characters and objects move in the scene in relation to the frame. In describing screen direction, you might say that a character is moving camera left vs camera right. Screen left and camera left mean the same thing in the context of screen direction

Cinematography:

Cinematography is the art of photography and visual storytelling in a motion picture or television show. Cinematography comprises all on-screen visual elements, including lighting, framing, composition, camera motion, camera angles, film selection, lens choices, depth of field, zoom, focus, color, exposure, and filtration

Editing :

In the film society, editing refers to the process of selecting and arranging the shots captured during the production phase to create a coherent and visually compelling story. The editor works closely with the director and the cinematographer to choose the best takes, cut together the footage, and apply visual and sound effects to enhance the overall impact of the film. The editing process can significantly affect the pacing, tone, and emotional resonance of the movie and is an essential aspect of post-production. Skilled editors use a variety of techniques, including continuity editing, montage, and match cuts, to create a seamless and immersive viewing experience.
